### **Tip 1: Learn Your Local Housing Market**

If you're thinking about buying a house in the near future, it's important to take the time to learn more about your local housing market. The price of homes can vary greatly depending on where you live. And because [prices are constantly changing](https://www.forbes.com/advisor/mortgages/real-estate/buying-your-first-home-ways-to-be-prepared/){data-affiliate="612"}, it's important to make sure you know what they are before making an offer on a home.

The following tips will help you learn more about your local housing market and prepare for your search:

-Research housing trends in your area by reading articles from local newspapers and websites

-Ask friends and family members who live in the area for information about their home purchase process

-Find out what kind of homes are available in your desired location

-Talk with people who work at real estate agencies or have bought homes in your desired location

-Visit open houses or new [home developments](https://www.coursesforsuccess.com/products/property-developer){data-affiliate="613"}

### **Tip 2: Find out your loan options**

As a first-time home buyer, you need to find out what types of loans are available for you. There are many types of loans and some may be more suitable for your needs than others. The two most common loans for first-time home buyers are the FHA loan and the VA loan.

According to the experts at [coolehome.com](https://coolehome.com/){data-affiliate="614"}, the FHA loan is a government-insured program that offers low down payment requirements, flexible credit guidelines, and competitive rates. The VA Loan is a government-guaranteed program that provides benefits to military veterans who meet eligibility criteria.

There are also many professional mortgage companies that can help guide you through this process as well as offer additional services such as pre-approvals, pre-qualifications, and mortgage insurance quotes.

### **Tip 3: Determine Affordability & Location with the 30/50/20 Rule**

The 30/50/20 rule is a guideline that determines affordability and location. The 30/50/20 rule states that the minimum down payment for a home should be at least 30% of the purchase price, the monthly mortgage payments should not exceed 50% of one’s monthly income, and finally, the [total housing costs](https://www.investopedia.com/updates/first-time-home-buyer/){data-affiliate="615"} (property taxes and insurance) should not exceed 20% of one’s gross income.

This rule has been used by many first-time home buyers to determine whether they can afford a house in their desired location.

### **Tip 4. Know the Difference Between Prime and Non-Prime Real Estate**

Prime real estate is a term for the most desirable and expensive properties in any given area.

The term "prime real estate" is often used to refer to the most desirable, valuable, or expensive property in a given market. This can be due to its location, size, or other factors that make it more appealing than other properties.

It's important for first-time buyers to understand the difference between prime and non-prime real estate so that they can think of it as a good investment as well.
