### Electric Showers

Electric showers are a popular pick for many households! They are **easy to install** and connect directly to the mains water supply. Plus, they have **adjustable temperature settings**. Safety features such as **thermal cut-outs and pressure relief devices** are also present.

A great bonus? You won't have to worry about other hot water usage in your home affecting your shower. As electric showers only heat the water used for each session, energy waste is reduced and you save on bills.

For maximum comfort and satisfaction, consider installing an electric shower with separate controls for **flow rate** and **temperature adjustment**. This way, you can customize your showering experience!

### Water Pressure

When picking a shower for your bathroom, water pressure is key! It decides the intensity and power of the flow, offering a great showering experience.

- **Check your house's water pressure before selecting a shower.**

- **If it's low, you may need a power shower or one with a pump.**

- **High-pressure showers are suitable for different showerheads, like rain and waterfall showers.**

- **Pick a showerhead that can manage water pressure variations if they happen.**

- **Certain showers come with pressure-regulating valves, so you can adjust the water pressure to your liking.**

- **A booster pump is worthy of investment if your water pressure is mostly low.**

To make sure you get the best showering experience, make sure the shower you choose is compatible with your home's water system. Think about your needs and preferences when it comes to water pressure, too.

**Pro Tip: If you don't know your home's water pressure, call a professional plumber to check it.**

### Energy Efficiency

Energy efficiency matters when selecting a shower. It helps cut energy use and contributes to environmental protection. Here are the key points for energy efficiency to keep in mind:

- Choose showers with low-flow or aerating showerheads. **They lower water consumption while still providing a good shower experience**.

- Look for showers with adjustable flow settings – **this lets you control the water flow, reducing wastage**.

- Opt for showers with built-in thermostatic controls. **They regulate the water temperature, cutting energy waste**.

- Go for showers with features like timers or sensors that automatically turn off after use. **This helps save water and energy**.

- Choose showers with LED lights. **They look great and use less electricity than traditional lamps**.

- If possible, select showers powered by renewable energy sources such as solar panels or hydroelectric power stations. **This further lowers your environmental impact**.

Plus, installing flow restrictors amplifies energy efficiency. These devices limit hot water flow through the showerhead, cutting down on water and energy without impacting comfort.

A family that was worried about their high water and energy bills decided to upgrade to a low-flow showerhead. The savings on their bills while still having a great shower were amazing! This made them realize the importance of considering energy efficiency in everyday decisions.
