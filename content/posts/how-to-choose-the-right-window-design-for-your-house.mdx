Relaxing indoors with a beautiful view is the best thing ever. The warm sunlight pouring in helps to bring the outdoors inside. Choosing the right windows for your self-build, addition, or remodel can be one of your most important design decisions. These helpful hints on selecting the best window design for your home can be of benefit if you're thinking about making a change from your current setup.

## Ventilation Needs

A window's primary function is to allow fresh air to enter and leave a room. The [questions to ask your window replacement contractor](https://www.simpsonwindowsanddoors.ca/blog/questions-you-should-be-asking-your-window-replacement-contractor-in-newmarket/){data-affiliate="766"} should cover what kind of window will best achieve this in your space. Whatever you end up with will depend to a large extent on your preferences and needs: bay windows, French casement windows, and the soaringly open airiness of tilt and turn and vertical sliding windows are all options. Refrain from assuming that bay and bow windows are exclusively found in older homes; they can also work in newer homes. Because of the increased number of panes, they allow abundant natural light into the room without sacrificing privacy.

## Frame Color

Frames that add color to the exterior of your home are a great way to customize your windows to your [home's design](https://fifti-fifti.net/living-room/what-interior-design-style-you-should-go-with-based-on-your-personality/){data-affiliate="767"}. Many homeowners will paint the window and door frames the same color as the trim around the house. There is no end to the variety of vibrant frames available to spruce up your home's decor. Shades like chartreuse, navy, and fiery red never go out of style.

## Windows That Can Be Opened In Different Styles

The traditional sash window is commonly used in homes, allowing the movable pane of glass to slide vertically up and down. Modern homes with sleek, minimalist designs benefit significantly from the space-saving advantages of tilt-and-turn windows. In addition to helping with those issues, they're also low maintenance, secure, quiet, and efficient. Our revolutionary French casement windows are another option for letting natural [light](https://fifti-fifti.net/living-room/living-room-lighting/){data-affiliate="768"} and fresh air into your home. These windows can be opened to a full 90 degrees, providing the best possible ventilation and views and the highest possible level of safety and [security](https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbes-personal-shopper/2021/10/08/home-security-systems/?sh=20b6b40b38d9){data-affiliate="769"}. It is essential to install a locking mechanism on your windows.

## The Sun's Position

It's important to think about where the sun will rise and set when working [with a builder or architect](https://designplusdrafting.com.au/how-much-does-an-architect-cost/){data-affiliate="770"} on a home's layout. Depending on your location, the sun could shine into your living room while watching TV in the late afternoon or wake you up too early in the morning. Considering the sun’s position can avoid these issues and help you [reduce your energy consumption](https://www.huffpost.com/entry/10-energy-saving-tips-to_b_6141678){data-affiliate="771"} throughout the more expensive heating and cooling seasons.

The first step in selecting the proper windows is ensuring they fit in with the distinctive architectural style of the house.
