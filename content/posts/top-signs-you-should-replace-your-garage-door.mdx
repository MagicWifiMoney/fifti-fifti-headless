Your garage door is one of your home's most crucial features. It protects your garage against the elements and provides security for your car and auxiliary equipment. Since it can take up to 30%t of your home's exterior, how your garage door looks can hugely contribute to your [home's value](https://www.bankrate.com/real-estate/how-much-is-my-house-worth/){data-affiliate="669"} and aesthetic appeal when you decide to sell. It's critically important to have your door regularly serviced and maintained to ensure it's always in the best condition possible.

While you should make repairs to your garage door to ensure it's always functional, there are times that you may need to replace it with a new one entirely. However, most homeowners fail to notice issues with their garage doors until signs of chronic wear and tear are quite evident. You should routinely assess your garage door's condition to identify the best time to replace it.You can find a wide selection of high-quality garage doors from a reputable supplier like [Doors Plus](https://www.doorsplus.com.au/){data-affiliate="670"}, to suit your unique taste and needs. If your garage door is beyond repair, these experts will give you viable alternatives, including the style, material, and price. Here are some vital signs that you need to replace your existing garage door:

## **1. Your Door Has Damage**

The most obvious sign you may need a new garage door is the visible wear and tear. This can include dents, cracks, rust, and [faded paint](https://fifti-fifti.net/patio/5-tips-for-the-perfect-finish-when-painting-your-patio-furniture/){data-affiliate="671"}. Once you notice these signs, don't hesitate, it's time to start planning for a replacement. Look for damaged hardware such as springs, tracks, and pulleys. Hardware degrades that happen over time could potentially render your garage door dysfunctional if the critical door components are not working properly. If your garage door has issues with these components, you should consider contacting a garage door company to get your new door installed properly.

## **2. Your Door Isn't Opening or Closing**

If your garage door does not open or close properly, it's time you consider a replacement. As stated here at [https://righttrackdoor.com](https://righttrackdoor.com){data-affiliate="672"}, such issues could arise due to broken tracks or springs bent out of alignment. Regardless of the root cause, if your garage door won't open and close with ease, it's not fulfilling its intended purpose. This is also true if your door makes unwanted strange noises, such as grinding and creaking. If you're not convinced whether to replace your garage door, don't hesitate to consult an experienced professional who will help you choose a good quality garage door that will last you for years.

## **3. Your Energy Costs Are Increasing**

Your old garage door might not be as well insulated as the newer door models. This translates to heat escaping from your home when you open and close your garage door. You'll find this to be especially true during summer and winter when energy costs are considerably higher. If your utility bill has been on a tremendous increase and you still don't know why it could be time you considered the long overdue garage door replacement.

## **Upgrade Your Garage Today**

If you see any of the signs listed above, maybe it's time to go shopping for a new garage door. Consulting a professional garage door technician will help you ascertain if it can be repaired or needs to be replaced entirely. Garage doors come in different sizes, so it's crucial that you take measurements before proceeding with the purchase. Additionally, you need to decide the features you want in your new garage door, making your home more welcoming and livable.
